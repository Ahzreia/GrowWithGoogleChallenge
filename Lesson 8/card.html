Amur Leopard

Leopards can leap up to 20 feet horizontally and 10 feet vertically. That's high enough to jump over an average-sized car! ~Pittsburgh Zoo

  • Scientific Name: Pantera pardus orientalis
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years
  • Conservation Status: Critcally Endangered
  • Other Names: Far Eastern leopard

Few Amur Lepoards live in the wild in the Russian Province of Primosky Krai. Amur Leopards differ from other leopards because they are the only leopard subspecies adapted to a cold snowy climate. They have a thick coat of spot-covered fur, long limbs, and are well adapted to walkin through deep snow.
